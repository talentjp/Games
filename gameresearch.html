The Efficiency Frontier

Data from thousands of Steam releases reveals a crucial insight: more art does not equal more money.

The "Golden Zone" represents genres where the median revenue is high, but the asset production requirement is low. These are games driven by systems, loops, and hooks rather than high-fidelity 3D models.

Key Insight:

Platformers are saturated and asset-heavy. Strategy and Simulation games offer a better Ratio of Return on Assets (ROA).

Design Difficulty

**Deckbuilders** require intense mathematical balancing. One overpowered card breaks the game. **Survivor-likes** are easier to code but hard to make "feel" good without juicy feedback.

Market Saturation

**Survivor-likes** are currently flooding the market. **Management Sims** have a stickier, more dedicated audience but a higher barrier to entry for development.

Resource Allocation

In a traditional Platformer or RPG, up to 60% of development time is spent on Asset Production (Art, Animation, Level Layout).
